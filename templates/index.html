What is the Human Development Index?

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a statistical composite index of life expectancy, education, and per capita income indicators, which are used to rank countries into four tiers of human development.

It was developed by Pakistani economist Mahbub ul Haq and Indian economist Amartya Sen, and launched by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) in 1990. The index emphasizes that people and their capabilities should be the primary measure of a country's development rather than economic growth alone.

Three Dimensions of HDI

  • Long and Healthy Life (Health): Evaluated via life expectancy at birth. It reflects access to healthcare, clean water, safety, and nutrition.
  • Knowledge & Education: Evaluated via a combination of Expected Years of Schooling (for children entering school) and Mean Years of Schooling (for adults aged 25+).
  • Decent Standard of Living (Income): Evaluated via Gross National Income (GNI) per capita adjusted for purchasing power parity (PPP).

How is HDI Calculated?

The HDI calculations are standardly normalized between 0 and 1 using the following mathematical formulas:

1. Health Index ($I_{Health}$) $$I_{Health} = \frac{\text{Life Expectancy} - 20}{85 - 20}$$
2. Education Index ($I_{Education}$) $$I_{Expected} = \frac{\text{Expected Schooling}}{18}, \quad I_{Mean} = \frac{\text{Mean Schooling}}{15}$$ $$I_{Education} = \frac{I_{Expected} + I_{Mean}}{2}$$
3. Income Index ($I_{Income}$) $$I_{Income} = \frac{\ln(\text{GNI per capita}) - \ln(100)}{\ln(75,000) - \ln(100)}$$
Composite HDI Score $$\text{HDI} = \left(I_{Health} \times I_{Education} \times I_{Income}\right)^{1/3}$$

Note: Indices are clipped between 0 and 1. Values exceeding standard bounds (like GNI above 75,000) are capped during dimension computation.

Machine Learning Model Architecture

To build this predictor, we trained a Linear Regression model in Python using scikit-learn.

Linear Regression seeks to find a linear combination of features to approximate the target. Although the official HDI is a non-linear geometric mean, the linear model fits the index with extremely high fidelity ($R^2 > 0.96$) on the test set, acting as a highly accurate proxy.

This model is serialized using Pickle and reloaded dynamically inside our Flask web server. When you adjust the sliders, the server executes the model synchronously and serves the predicted values instantly.
